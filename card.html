Polar Bear

Polar bears can swim constantly for days at a time.

  • Scientific Name: Ursus maritimus
  • Average Length: 8-10 feet
  • Average Lifespan: 25-30 years
  • Habitat: Arctic

Polar bears are stocky, with a long neck, relatively small head, short, rounded ears, and a short tail. The male, which is much larger than the female, weighs 410 to 720 kg (900 to 1,600 pounds). It grows to about 1.6 metres (5.3 feet) tall at the shoulder and 2.2–2.5 metres in length.

Koala

Koalas can smell like eucalytpus as they spend most of their time sitting in the trees.

  • Scientific Name: Phascolarctos cinereus
  • Average Length: 71 cm
  • Average Lifespan: 13-18 years
  • Habitat: Australian forests

Koalas are well-known marsupials that live in Australia. Most people can easily recognize these mammals by their greyish fur, fluffy ears, and large black noses. Though some people call them “Koala bears,” they are not related to bears in any way, shape, or form! In fact, their closest relative is the wombat.

Sea Turtle

Sea turtles don't retract into their shells like other turtles.

  • Scientific Name: Chelonioidea
  • Average Length: 3 feet
  • Average Lifespan: 50-100 years
  • Habitat: Shallow, coastal waters + bays

Sea turtles are large, air-breathing reptiles that inhabit tropical and subtropical seas throughout the world. Sea turtles come in many different sizes, shapes and colors. Sea turtles do not have teeth, but their jaws have modified “beaks” suited to their particular diet. They do not have visible ears but have eardrums covered by skin.
